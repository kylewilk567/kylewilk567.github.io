About me

I am a junior studying Computer Science with a minor in Electrical Engineering at the University of Michigan. My grandpa and my father studied at U of M, so I felt propelled to follow in their footsteps. When I first came to college, I did not know what I wanted to do. I had always loved math and science as a kid (having learned to count money by age 4), but I did not find my love for programming until shortly after my freshman year when I began programming in Minecraft for fun. I quickly preferred programming to actually playing the game itself! What I most like about programming is the necessity of breaking down a complex problem into tiny parts that a computer can understand and the satisfaction that comes from a working program. I am eager to keep learning about algorithms to use as tools to solve complex problems as well as make existing programs more efficient. My first goal is to learn more about machine learning. You can read more about the related project (FDM 3D Printing Fault Prediction) below.
